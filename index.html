Culture Rating

Reference: Got your Culture right here!

The culture rating is based on the access to education, entertainment, health, and religion. Several formulas depend on a coverage percentage, which is computed differently for each category. These percentages are reflected by the messages in the various overseer windows. Note that the “Average” rating covers a wider band than the others.

The coverage percentage for health (physician, dentist, mortuary) is the simplest. It refers to the percentages of houses that have access to that service. The other coverages (entertainment, religion, education) take into account the number of service buildings built and don't care about how many houses have access to the service.

Desirability: The number in the D1 column represents the desirability effect (positive or negative) that the Structure has on adjacent squares. These values are the same for all difficulty levels.

Reference: Musings on Production and Walker Speeds

In the tables below, speed is how much the production increases for each game tick. Production completes when it gets over 100%. There are 16 ticks per month and 192 ticks per year. Ptah's temple complex and altar can speed up some kinds of production by 50%. A cart pusher can walk 3.33 tiles per tick.

Based on this we can compute how much a building will produce per year and the maximum delivery distance so that the cart pusher returns before the next load is complete.

Formulas

ticks  = ceil(100/speed)
yearly = 100 * 192/ticks
dist   = floor(ticks*1.7)

Services

Libraries, mortuaries, etc, consume goods every time they generate a walker. The yearly consumption will depend on how many trips the walker makes, which in turn depends on your block size and city layout. For a standard-sized block, expect about 9 walks per year. For the tiniest blocks, there can be up to 12 walks per year. The oracle of Toth reduces consumption of papyrus and the oracle of Anubis reduces the consumption of linen. Houses

Houses consume pottery, beer, linen, and luxury goods at a rate of 12 units per year for 1×1 houses and 24 units per year for large houses. The storage capacity is 2 months for small houses and 4 months for large houses.

Houses consume food at a rate of 3 food per person per year. This can be reduced to 2.4 food per person per year if you have a temple complex to Bast or an Altar to Sebek (which is part of the temple complex to Osiris). Houses can hold enough food for 2 years, for each kind of food. If there are two kinds of food, this means a 4 year supply, and for three kinds of food it's a 6 year supply.

Houses consume food in this order of preference:

  1. Grain
  2. Meat
  3. Lettuce
  4. Chickpeas
  5. Pomegranates
  6. Figs
  7. Fish
  8. Game Meat
